Executive Summary

Kelly Strayhorn Theater (KST) is a performing arts center located in the East Liberty neighborhood of Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. KST has faced barriers in terms of their ability to reach new audiences, so our team has developed a digital showcase to cross-promote KST programming, both in-person and virtual, through partnerships with local Pittsburgh businesses.

Final Product Overview

KST Passport, a gamified rewards program, will increase KST’s name recognition among local community members and broaden their active base of attendees.

Statement of Problem

Most people in Pittsburgh don’t know about KST and what it has to offer to the local community. With the COVID-19 pandemic, KST has faced barriers in terms of their ability to reach new audiences.
"How do we bridge the gap and encourage participation from ordinary community members at KST?"

With COVID-19, the Kelly Strayhorn Theater has had to adapt to changing circumstances and audiences. We’ve challenged ourselves to find out how people are staying safe, while enjoying entertainment and the performing arts.

Methods

We conducted our research using directed storytelling, speed dating, experience prototyping, along with contextual activities over Zoom to understand how people perceive and engage with collaborations, how they have been consuming content during the pandemic, and how they have connecting with local businesses.
Affinity Diagramming: Our synthesized results from an interpretation session after conducting contextual inquiries using directed storytelling.
Storyboarding & Speed Dating: Our affinity diagram led us to sketch out possible solutions that we user-tested via "speed dating" sessions.
Experience Prototyping: After eliminating lackluster storyboards, we began lofi wireframing a final solution while iterating over feedback.

Contextual Inquiry: How We Gathered Participant Data

We conducted our research using directed storytelling with contextual activities over Zoom to understand how people perceive and engage with collaborations, how they have been consuming content during the pandemic, and how they have connecting with local businesses. Our insights tackle the crux of community involvement and audience engagement in the performing arts, suggesting areas for opportunity to appeal to both potential theater attendees to try new events and regular fans to feel more involved through community collaboration.

Storyboarding + Speed Dating Interviews: Research Plan

Each team member recruited one participant for an interview that would be conducted via remotely via Zoom. Our prototype was hosted on Invision and we created hotspots so that we could easily click through the prototype without interrupting the flow. The interview started with a brief introduction of our project, then we warmed up the participant with some icebreaker questions to get them thinking in the context of reward programs. After the icebreaker, we dove into the prototype and discussion prompts. Some time was left at the end for questions and comments.

Experience Prototyping: User Testing Method

In order to test our prototype, we settled on using the Wizard of Oz method to test our low-fidelity prototype with participants. Our context of use focuses on interacting a local business, but since that is not currently possible, we decided to set up the scenario using a storytelling method and have the participants test the app as members of the rewards program. Since we were still in a more exploratory phase of the project, we also prompted the user with questions to encourage them to talk about the events and promotions that would most likely pique their interest and keep them coming back to the reward program.

Insights & Evidence Gathered

Based on the research methods and exercises performed, we found various pieces of evidence that supported and led us to our final solution.
“I trust collaborations when I know at least one of the two creators.”
“For me, the most important thing is saving money. If I’m saving money while getting to support my local theater and restaurants, then definitely I’ll be doing that.”
One of our storyboards generated particularly positive feedback and aligned with the other insights gathered from previous methods.

Insight 1

Recommendations from trusted friends and connections to familiar entities are key to engaging in an unfamiliar experience.

Insight 2

People are more likely to discover new brands through collaborations with their favorite brands and businesses.

Insight 3

Participation in the local community aligns with individual interests and is largely impacted by cost, convenience, and accessibility.

“I think if there’s a way for me to kill two birds with one stone and stay engaged with my community while feeling like I’m not breaking the bank, then I’m very down to try it out."
"Everyone is bored and stuck at home, and looking for things to do..."
