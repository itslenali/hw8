About me

I design, research, and prototype desirable, feasible, and viable interactive products. Currently, I am studying at Carnegie Mellon University, working on my strategic design and human-centered design skillsets.

In the past, I designed products that use big data to aid public health policy-making for the US CDC (Centers for Disease Control and Prevention), ASEAN (Association of Southeast Asian Nations) and WHO (World Health Organization). During the Zika outbreak, my data visualizations appeared in major news outlets and academic journals such as The Economist, The Washington Post, and The Lancet Infectious Diseases. In the past, my research work concentrated on creating

interactive and immersive data visualization experiences for social good. These projects have been featured on the Creators Project, City Pulse 24, Canadian Broadcasting Corporation. They have been published in MIT's Leonardo and at ACM SIGCHI Conference. I am constantly learning and looking for a challenge, feel free to reach out if you are looking to connect.
